# Suffolk Customer Story

This is the week-one story we want Suffolk Restoration Group to feel:

- Service Butler is a calm, premium operating layer.
- It surfaces real demand early.
- It keeps the queue small and actionable.
- It shows why each lead matters.
- It turns work into booked jobs that can be traced back.

## Positioning line

**Service Butler helps Suffolk Restoration Group see real jobs earlier, work the right leads faster, and prove which signals became revenue.**

## What it is not

- Not another noisy inbox
- Not a generic CRM replacement
- Not a dashboard full of graphs nobody trusts
- Not a lead dump

## What it should feel like

- Clear
- Premium
- Fast
- Obvious
- Trustworthy
- Calm under pressure

## Week-one value story

The customer should understand three things immediately:

1. We found a real signal before the team would have seen it another way.
2. We showed exactly why it deserved attention.
3. We made it easy to accept, reject, or act on the lead.

## Customer-facing proof outputs

In week one, the customer should be shown:

- A short lead list with only the most relevant opportunities
- A reason code for every accepted or rejected lead
- The source and provenance behind each good lead
- One booked-job example tied back to the original signal
- A weekly summary that says what to keep, tune, or pause

## Operator workflow

The daily workflow should feel simple:

1. Open the lead queue.
2. Review the top items first.
3. Accept or reject with a reason code.
4. Call or text the best leads.
5. Mark the outcome.

The weekly workflow should feel equally simple:

1. Review what came in.
2. Review what was accepted.
3. Review what booked.
4. Review what should change next week.

## What the customer should trust

- Territory fit
- Service-line fit
- Contactability
- Reasonable urgency
- Booked-job traceability

## What to say in customer meetings

- "This is your early-warning system for real service demand."
- "We are not trying to overwhelm your team; we are trying to surface the right work."
- "Here is what mattered this week and why."
- "Here is what turned into booked revenue."
- "Here is what we are changing based on your feedback."

## Week-one success criteria

- The customer says the queue is useful, not noisy.
- The customer can explain why a lead was accepted or rejected.
- The customer sees at least one lead-to-booked-job example.
- The customer understands which sources are helping.
- The customer wants to keep using it into week two.
