# Suffolk Onboarding Checklist

Use this checklist to onboard the first Suffolk restoration customer into a safe pilot.

## Before kickoff

- Confirm customer name, primary contacts, and escalation contacts.
- Confirm the target operator scope: one brand, one or more territories, one weekly review cadence.
- Confirm which team members will use the system: owner, dispatcher, lead reviewer, outbound owner.
- Confirm the pilot start date and business hours for active coverage.
- Confirm that the customer agrees to safe outbound mode until approved otherwise.

## System access

- Confirm the customer has working login credentials.
- Confirm the operator tenant is seeded and visible in the system.
- Confirm the customer can see the correct territories, leads, and jobs.
- Confirm the customer knows where to find scanner, leads, pipeline, jobs, and reporting views.

## Workflow orientation

- Show the customer the lead journey: signal, opportunity, lead, outreach, booked job, attribution.
- Show the customer how to accept or reject a lead.
- Show the customer where to see lead provenance and why the lead was scored.
- Show the customer how to mark a lead as booked, disqualified, or needing follow-up.
- Show the customer how weekly proof-of-value reporting will work.

## Safety and controls

- Explain that outbound defaults to safe mode until the customer approves live traffic.
- Explain how opt-outs and suppression work.
- Explain how to escalate a bad lead, bad routing decision, or integration issue.
- Explain who to contact if a webhook, alert, or booking fails.

## Go-live readiness

- The customer can log in successfully.
- The customer can see at least one territory and one lead queue.
- The customer knows how to accept, reject, and escalate a lead.
- The customer knows how to review booked-job attribution.
- The customer knows the weekly review time and support contact.

## Day-one handoff

- Send the customer a copy of the runbook pack.
- Confirm the next check-in time.
- Confirm who owns daily lead review.
- Confirm who owns follow-up calls and outbound actions.
